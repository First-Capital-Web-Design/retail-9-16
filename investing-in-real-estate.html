REAL ESTATE AS AN ASSET CLASS

While stocks and bonds have their place as traditional instruments of investment, investors are increasingly considering alternative investments such as real estate, hedge funds, private equity and exchange traded funds - in an attempt to engineer overall enhanced performance of their portfolios. Historically, real estate has been relatively un-correlated to the broader stock and bond market. Said differently, investments in real estate may not fluctuate as much as other investments based on the broader stock market, and therefore we believe real estate can help to diversify a portfolio. We also believe real estate can provide an investor with the opportunity to earn current cash flow, while possibly serving as a hedge against inflation. We believe that real estate is an important component of any well diversified portfolio. We also believe that, unless investors have the experience and knowledge to actively manage real property assets, they will be better served investing with managers with a proven track record. While there are fees associated with professionally managed REITs, we believe that the cost can be offset by the benefit of an experienced manager.

Real estate is an integral part of our economy, and represents more than one-third of the value of all capital asset investments in the U.S. Real estate has historically provided investors the opportunity to earn current cash flow, while at times serving as a hedge against inflation.

TRANSPARENCY

Historically, most non-traded REITs have not provided daily valuations for their shares. In an effort to provide a daily valuation of our Common Shares, we will daily establish our net asset value, or NAV, per Common Share, following the "NAV pricing start date," which refers to the earliest to occur of: (a) our investing in assets with an aggregate cost, including our pro rata share of debt attributable to such assets, in excess of $1 billion; (b) our raising net offering proceeds of in excess of $650 million in our primary offering; and (c) the date that is 29 months following the commencement of our initial public offering. Following the NAV pricing start date, we will: (1) file a monthly pricing supplement, which will identify the NAV per Common Share for such month, with the Securities and Exchange Commission; (2) post the pricing supplement on our website; and (3) make the daily NAV per Common Share available to investors on our toll-free, automated telephone line at (855) REIT-NAV. Our advisor, rather than an independent party, will calculate our NAV, based in part on the appraisals of our properties performed by an independent valuer and in accordance with the valuation guidelines established by our board of directors.

LIQUIDITY

While investments in real estate are generally not liquid, and while investors in our company should consider their investment as "locked-in" for a number of years, we recognize that there are times when investors may need liquidity. For that reason we have established a policy, following the NAV pricing start date, to maintain sufficient cash or liquid investments so that we can redeem shares up to 20% of our NAV in any 12-month period, subject to the amount of cash or liquid investments available for such purposes. Shareholders may make daily requests that we redeem their shares, but we may not be able to fulfill repurchase requests. Prior to the NAV pricing start date, shareholders may redeem their shares in the case of hardship. Following the NAV pricing start date, shareholders may make daily requests that we redeem shares at 95% of our published Daily NAV per Common Share. The share repurchase program may be limited, modified or suspended at the discretion of the Board of Directors.

OWN REAL ESTATE WITHOUT HAVING TO OPERATE IT

We believe that investors should have real estate in their portfolios - but not exposure to the headaches of property ownership. While the benefits of owning real estate may be desirable, the management of property may not. By investing with First Capital Real Estate Trust Incorporated, you can indirectly own real estate without the managerial headaches, and you can get access to larger commercial real estate properties not always accessible to smaller investors and have that property professionally managed with a goal of generating the best returns possible.

IS THIS THE RIGHT TIME FOR REAL ESTATE?

Knowing when to get into an investment and when to get out is an important key to success. Some may wonder if now is the right time to get into real estate. While it is true that there has been a general decline in real property values, it is our view that we are approaching an ideal opportunity to acquire properties now. The recent decline in prices makes it possible for us to acquire properties for considerably less than it would have cost just 3 years ago, often at below replacement cost. We are also in a historically low interest rate environment. That means that we can borrow today at greatly reduced interest rates - among the lowest ever! Leverage is one of the components that make real estate so attractive. And over the next few years, we believe, several other trends will occur that make the opportunity even better. By 2013 an unprecedented number of securitized loans will be coming due at debt levels that will be difficult, if not impossible, to refinance. That should create a further buying opportunity for smart real estate investors. Additionally, it is our view that based on the global sovereign debt crisis, governments will be forced to take actions, which will result in an inflationary cycle in the mid to long term. For all these reasons, we believe we can acquire properties at great prices, finance them with low interest rate debt, improve the value of the rental stream as inflation starts to kick-in, and then sell the assets at higher prices towards the end of our holding period. If our view is correct, now is the time to be investing in real estate.
